# M-4 (PROPOSED) — Canonical Question Ingestion & Consolidation

**Status: DRAFT v0.1 — NOT AN APPROVED METHOD.**
This is an outline for founder review (FD-11). It carries no authority,
gates its own implementation, and becomes M-4 only when explicitly frozen.
Until then, no canonical question may be produced by it or any other
procedure.

**Date:** July 14, 2026
**Purpose:** the auditable procedure that turns raw observed expressions
into canonical questions without letting editorial invention re-enter
through the consolidation step.

---

## 1. What counts as an observed expression

A verbatim interaction artifact, captured at the moment of observation:

- a raw prompt submitted to an evaluator environment
- a search query
- a buyer's wording in an interview or conversation
- an evaluator's own follow-up or decomposition question
- an interview statement expressing a commercial uncertainty

**Admissibility requirements (all mandatory):** verbatim text (no
paraphrase, no reconstruction from memory, no AI-generated summary of an
expression); capture timestamp; evaluator-environment identity (EE-);
capturing instrument version (I-); source method; consent state for
human-derived sources. An artifact missing any of these is not an
observed expression and cannot support a commercial question.

**Explicitly inadmissible:** hearsay ("buyers often ask…"), recollected
paraphrase, expressions generated by us to illustrate a category,
AI-model output *invented on request* as "typical questions" (that is an
evaluator behavior observation about the model, not evidence of market
demand).

## 2. Source types and quality requirements

Source methods (durable vocabulary; platforms are EE-registry data):
`evaluator-prompt`, `evaluator-followup`, `search-data`, `interview`,
`customer-conversation`, `internal-research`, `finding-derived`.

Per-method quality floors:

- **search-data:** query verbatim + impression/frequency data + date
  window; the property and export method recorded.
- **evaluator-prompt / followup:** full session capture per the
  instrument's protocol; raw output stored as content-hashed Evidence.
- **interview / customer-conversation:** verbatim quotation with consent
  state; anonymization declared per the named-parties rule; paraphrase
  marked as paraphrase and inadmissible for consolidation support.

## 3. Raw permanence

Observed expressions are immutable and preserved permanently. PII
redaction is performed on capture with redactions declared (what was
removed and why). **No canonicalization step may edit, delete, or
overwrite an expression.** Splits, merges, and re-classifications re-point
edges; they never touch the raw layer.

## 4. Clustering — how variants are grouped

1. Candidate grouping by shared underlying uncertainty, not by lexical
   similarity.
2. **The resolution test:** two expressions belong to the same candidate
   cluster only if a complete answer to one would fully resolve the other
   for the same buying role at the same stage.
3. **Default-to-separate:** expressions remain separate unless an
   affirmative consolidation case is documented. Doubt keeps them apart.
4. Expressions differing in buying role, buyer stage, requirement under
   evaluation, or intent stay separate even when lexically near-identical.
5. Every clustering decision records: coder identity, date, the
   resolution-test reasoning, and the M-4 version applied.

## 5. Underlying-uncertainty assignment

Each cluster maps to exactly one `uncertainty` (UN-) with written
rationale; the uncertainty links to the requirement or gate it concerns
(or a new requirement is proposed, itself evidence-backed). If no
uncertainty can be articulated, the cluster is not ready to canonicalize.

## 6. Canonical wording selection

- The canonical wording must be **entailed by the supporting
  expressions** — it may generalize their surface forms but may not add
  scope, vocabulary, or framing absent from all of them.
- Prefer the structure most frequent among expressions; neutral
  vocabulary; vendor or product names only when present across supporting
  expressions.
- Wording is versioned; rewording is a recorded revision.

## 7. When two expressions remain separate

Codified from §4: different resolution targets, roles, stages,
requirements, or intents — plus any case where coders disagree (§8).

## 8. Disagreement preservation

Competing canonicalizations are first-class: rival CQs linked by
`contests` edges with status `contested`, each keeping its own expression
support. Contests are resolved only by new evidence or a documented
methodological ruling — never by silent merge. The disagreement history
survives resolution.

## 9. Confidence coding

Consolidation confidence coded per CQ:

- **high** — ≥ N expressions from ≥ M distinct source methods, coder
  agreement on clustering, uncertainty assignment uncontested
- **medium** — threshold met on expressions but single source method, or
  minor coding disagreement resolved with documented reasoning
- **low** — minimum support only, or unresolved contest pending

(N and M are open parameters — §14.)

## 10. Replication requirements

Consolidation must be reproducible: an independent coder given the same
expression set should reach the same clusters at or above a target
agreement rate (parameter, §14). A sampled re-coding runs on a fixed
cadence; agreement rates are published as observatory instrument health.
While the program is single-coder, that fact is declared on every CQ and
self-agreement re-coding (time-separated) substitutes, honestly labeled.

## 11. Evaluator-environment capture

Every expression carries its EE- reference and the instrument version
that captured it; every derived evaluator-distribution statistic on a CQ
is computed from those stamps, never asserted.

## 12. Revision, split, and merge rules

- Splits and merges are Revision objects with reasons and impact.
- CQ IDs are never reused; superseded CQs remain published with forward
  pointers.
- Expressions re-point; the raw layer is untouched (§3).
- Class changes (e.g., editorial-navigation → observed-commercial upon
  external support) are revisions carrying the new expression evidence.

## 13. Safeguards against editorial invention

- The class system (QUESTION_OBSERVATORY §3): observed-commercial requires
  expression support — build-enforced; editorial questions carry visible
  labels and never enter market-coverage denominators.
- The entailment rule (§6): canonical wording can never exceed its
  expressions.
- The audit trail: every CQ resolves to verbatim expressions in a fixed
  number of clicks, for any skeptic, human or machine.
- A periodic laundering check: sampled CQs re-examined for wording that
  drifted beyond entailment; drift findings are published revisions.

## 14. Open parameters for the founder (must be set before freeze)

1. N (minimum expressions) and M (minimum distinct source methods) for
   each confidence grade and for observed-commercial admission.
2. Target coder-agreement rate and re-coding cadence.
3. Redaction and consent policy details for human-derived expressions.
4. Whether search-data frequency thresholds gate admission (a query seen
   once vs. a pattern).
