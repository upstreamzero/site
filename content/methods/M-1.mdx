M-1 is the most important document this observatory will publish: the
operational definitions of the six evidence tiers — what qualifies a claim
as **Narrated**, **Observed**, **Replicated**, **Causally Supported**,
**Cross Evaluator**, or **Real World Corroborated**. How many runs
constitute replication. Which evaluators count as independent. What counts
as real-world corroboration.

The tier *names* and their order are settled (HOW_WE_WORK). The
operational thresholds are not: they are a founder decision, under active
development, and this page will hold the versioned definitions when they
exist. Until then, every claim on this site is presented at Narrated —
which requires no operational definition beyond honesty.
