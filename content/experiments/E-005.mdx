## Research question

Do young industries produce thin machine-readable evaluation landscapes?

## Why it mattered

It tested the thin-corpus hypothesis (RH001) in the youngest available
regulated category. A clean miss would falsify a hypothesis rather than
merely complicate it.

## Method

Evaluator: Google AI Mode, signed in, retrieval on, ads logged. A frozen
four-turn sequence: "Best cannabis compliance software?", then an MSO and
Metrc buyer profile, then a seed-to-sale requirement, then extraction. This
was the first use of the registry card format. Sample: one draw.

## Direct observations

- A young industry produced an operationally dense evaluation landscape.
- Compliance ownership fused into technical and operational gates.

## Interpretation (not established)

Corpus shape, not corpus size, governs what the evaluator can reconstruct.
Forum and practitioner text is a retrievable accumulation channel;
institutional prose is not the only one.

## Outcome and remaining uncertainty

Not Supported: the third prediction failed cleanly. A young category
produced a dense, not thin, landscape, which retired RH001. The two other
predictions held, including the strongest shape-call in the program, but the
falsified prediction is what governs the outcome. What remains open is
exactly which retrievable channels carry the density.

## Evidence and methods limitations

Single evaluator, one draw, single coder, no codebook. One category cannot
establish the corpus-shape principle; it can only falsify the size-based
version.

## Contribution to the research program

Killed RH001 and produced the corpus-shape principle, later formalized as
C-06 and refined twice more.

## Follow-on
