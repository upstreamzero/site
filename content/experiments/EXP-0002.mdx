**Knowledge Reconstruction Fidelity.** The observatory's second
experiment asks a question that only becomes meaningful *after*
propagation: once an evaluator has recognized and retrieved Upstream Zero,
**can it reconstruct Upstream Zero faithfully:** its identity, claims,
relationships, evidence tiers, uncertainty, and theory? This is distinct
from [EXP-0001](/experiments/EXP-0001) (Knowledge Propagation), and the
two are not merged: recognition and retrieval are the precondition;
faithful reconstruction is the thing measured here.

**Day 0 evidence is not scored here.** Propagation captures collected at
Day 0 are baseline observations for EXP-0001. Reconstruction scoring
applies only to responses in which the evaluator has already recognized
and retrieved the entity; attaching Day 0 propagation captures to
reconstruction scoring would confound the two experiments.

**Motivating example (external, not evidence produced by Upstream
Zero):** the *Inspired Taste* case, in which visibility and citation
coexisted with a failure of representation fidelity. It demonstrates that
being cited is not the same as being reconstructed faithfully. That is the
exact pattern this experiment is built to measure. It motivates the question; it
evidences no finding of ours.

formally pre-registered. It has not been pre-registered, no data has been
collected, and its predictions are Narrated intuitions registered so that
