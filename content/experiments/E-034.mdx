## Research question

When a buyer profile and commercial requirements are introduced in sequence,
does the evaluator's recommendation field stay plural, or does it narrow?

## Why it mattered

Turn-one extraction shows a landscape. This run watched what happened to that
landscape as a buyer profile and real requirements were added across the
draws, in clinical trial management software (CTMS).

## Method

Evaluator: Google AI Mode, single surface. The run began from an unqualified
category recommendation, then introduced a buyer profile and commercial
requirements in sequence, observing how the recommendation field changed at
each step. Three draws. Instrument details and scoring are retained
internally.

## Direct observations

- The recommendation field narrowed from several vendors to a single final
  recommendation.
- Advarra OnCore was the vendor that persisted, surviving all three draws.
- The decisive narrowing appeared once the buyer profile was introduced,
  earlier in the sequence than the run anticipated.
- Under the tested condition, the plural field did not hold. It collapsed
  onto one vendor.

## Interpretation (not established)

In this category and on this surface, the opening recommendation reads as
weakly held: a plural landscape that reduces to a single vendor once a buyer
profile and requirements are present. Whether the early collapse is a
property of this category, this surface, this buyer profile, or
recommendation behavior more broadly is not established here.

## Outcome and remaining uncertainty

Supported: the recommendation field collapsed to a single vendor under the
tested condition, which is the outcome the run was built to detect. The scope
is narrow. One evaluator, one surface, three draws, one buyer profile, and no
removal test, so nothing here is causal. It does not generalize beyond this
category and these conditions on this evidence.

## Evidence and methods limitations

One evaluator, one surface (Google AI Mode), three draws, single coder. No
removal or flip test was run, so no causal claim is made about what forced
the collapse. The program still holds exactly one causally verified gate
(E-002), and this run does not add to it.

## Contribution to the research program

Recorded a category, CTMS, in which the recommendation field collapsed to a
single vendor once buyer context and requirements were present. The companion
category is E-040.

## Follow-on

E-040 ran a comparable sequence in project-management software and the field
did not collapse to one vendor. Whether the difference is driven by category
is an open question, not a finding.
