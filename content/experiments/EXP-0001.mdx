**Knowledge Propagation.** The observatory's first experiment observes
itself: *can evaluators discover, retrieve, and recognize a newly
published institutional knowledge system?* This site's own publication is
an intervention in the environment we study. Its objects may enter
evaluator retrieval and reasoning. You could call that contamination. We
measure it (Propagation Records), per the founder ruling that Client Zero
intentionally participates in the environment it studies.
Faithfulness of reconstruction *once* recognition and retrieval occur is a
separate experiment, [EXP-0002](/experiments/EXP-0002); Day 0 captures
here are propagation baselines only and are never scored for
reconstruction fidelity.

**Status honesty:** this is a draft protocol. It has not been
pre-registered, because pre-registration requires the founder-approved
final protocol and a public, timestamped record. The timestamped record now
exists in Git; the founder-approved final protocol does not yet. No data has
been collected. When
registration happens, this object's status change will be a recorded
Revision.
