To get recommended, a vendor has to survive a series of gates: getting
surfaced, passing screening, holding up in a comparison, clearing
validation, making the final cut. And once a recommendation exists, it has
to survive being re-asked, re-sampled, run on a newer model, and pushed on
with follow-up questions. How well a vendor holds up through all of that is
its recommendation survivability.

It's our term, and we haven't pinned down how to measure it yet. That's
part of M-1. We list it here so we're not leaning on a word we can't yet
define.
