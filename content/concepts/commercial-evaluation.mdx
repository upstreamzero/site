The process by which organizations evaluate products, services, vendors,
and technologies before selecting them. Buying committees apply
[requirements](/concepts/requirements); requirements shape confidence;
confidence shapes recommendations; recommendations shape selection. It has
always been economically enormous and methodologically invisible —
inferred only from its exhaust: win rates, lost deals, buyer anecdotes.

Commercial evaluation is the discipline; requirements are the invariant it
observes.

**How this differs from adjacent terms:** market research studies demand;
sales methodology studies persuasion; commercial evaluation is the
*decision process itself*, as an object of scientific study.
