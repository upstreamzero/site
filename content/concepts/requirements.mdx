Why do some vendors consistently make the shortlist while others are never
evaluated? Because every evaluation reduces to requirements — the specific
conditions a buyer needs satisfied in a given situation. An RFP is a
requirements list. A procurement workflow is a requirements filter. A
buying committee argues about which requirements matter. An AI system
screening vendors is matching candidates against inferred requirements.
Different interfaces, one underlying structure.

Requirements are what [commercial evaluation](/concepts/commercial-evaluation)
observes. Commercial evaluation is the discipline; requirements are the
invariant it studies.

That distinction is the reason an observatory can exist. Models change,
interfaces change, retrieval systems change — but the requirements a buyer
must see satisfied persist. Study the durable layer and findings outlast
the tools that surfaced them; study the tools and findings expire with
them.

**Status honesty:** the precise, measurable treatment of requirement
coverage is under development alongside [M-1](/methods/M-1). Requirements
are listed here because the discipline rests on them, not because the
measurement is finished.
